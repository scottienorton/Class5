Taeko Ohnuki is a Japanese Singer-songwriter who rose to popularity in the late seventies. Her music has helped define the city pop genre.

"There were these gaps that weren’t filled up by any rules.
I was able to experiment with interesting ideas back then...
Now, you can’t do that. Those gaps have been filled.."






Taeko formed a band called Sugar Babe in 1975
which combined elements of Jazz and Pop. Because this type of
music was not popular at the time, the band broke up. Her second
solo album, Sunshower, released in 1977, was a commercial failure
but would later garner critical acclaim. My favorite track is Tokai.

For her third album, Mignonne, Taeko worked with producer Eji Ogura.
Becuase the focus was more on sales, and the album wasn't as
successful as hoped, Taeko took a two year break from music.
Still, this album spawned the classic "4:00am", arguably her biggest commercial hit.
